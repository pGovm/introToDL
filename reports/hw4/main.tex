\section*{\textbf{Conclusion}}
From the resulting trained models, the Transformer found it easier to optimize the English-to-French translation direction. Beyond the higher BLEU scores achieved in the English-to-French direction across all configurations, the models also exhibited less overfitting compared to the French-to-English direction, as evidenced by lower validation losses. Training time measurements recorded in the notebook further support this, the Transformer consistently trained faster in the English-to-French direction. Both directions achieved a 0\% in exact sequence accuracy, which is expected given the complexity of character translation with a small dataset. However, BLEU scores consistently favor English-to-French across all six configurations, confirming that the Transformer finds this direction easier to optimize. An interesting thing to note is this contrasts with the RNN-based findings from Homework 3, where French-to-English was the easier direction.
